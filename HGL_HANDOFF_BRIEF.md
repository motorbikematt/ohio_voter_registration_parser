## Strategic Vision

**The Problem:** Precinct captains are the bottleneck in grassroots organizing. One person managing \~1,000 voters faces:

- Cognitive overload (voter file \+ Census \+ news \+ civic data \= too much, fragmented)  
- Blank-page problem (how to write messaging in own voice for their precinct?)  
- Risk aversion (door-knock conversations are high-impact but high-friction; easier to do low-impact lit drops)  
- Time scarcity (can't simultaneously research context \+ understand cohorts \+ craft messaging \+ manage logistics)

**Current behavior:** Precinct captains resort to low-friction, low-impact work (lit drops) because high-impact work (door-knock conversations) is high-friction.

**The Solution:** Four stacked agentic workflows that automate the precinct captain's day-to-day work:

1. **Data fusion agent** — Daily synthesis of Census \+ crime \+ civic \+ news data \+ voter file → one-page precinct brief (demographics, top 3 local issues, sentiment).  
     
2. **Content synthesis agent** — Curate newsletters (journalist/activist/government) \+ precinct brief → 3–5 message angles precinct captain can use in own voice. Daily "talking points" email.  
     
3. **Targeting/routing agent** — Voter cohort (Pure R/D, Crossover, UNC) \+ local issue \+ message variant → recommended households to visit today, in visit order, with conversation starter.  
     
4. **Conversation coaching agent** (Phase 5\) — Pre-knock context \+ live-during-knock mobile app \+ post-knock feedback loop. Continuous learning: which messages drive contact? Which cohorts persuadable on which issues?

## Customer Insight (From Precinct Captain Research)

**Founder research:** I have been a local activist for nearly a year and was formally elected precinct captain in Kettering 1-A as of May 2026\. I have showed this to 9 other precinct captains and 4 current and former candidates for state and local office. I have also shown this to two city council members.  My main collaborator is a decades long activist for the Democratic party. Their feedback:

- "The shyness factor is real — I'll do lit drops all day, but door knocks feel risky"  
- The biggest thing we need to do is convert lit-droppers to door knockers.  
- The party website is garbage, doesn’t work. The newsletters are inconsistent and often with last minute or expired information.   
- NGP Van app seems useful at first, but is not easy to use and the information is demonstrably outdated. Houses that show as Democrat are sometimes Trump supporters and that’s scary”  
- The Montgomery County Democratic party doesn’t have enough precinct captains across the county and uses that to their advantage to not share how they do business.  
- Many people do not vote because they don’t feel like the party or politicians care about them.   
- Many democrats have voted for Trump because they feel betrayed by the local party and politicians who only seem to care about themselves.  
- I live in a rural part of montgomery county but all I ever hear about is Dayton Dayton Dayton.   
- "I have no idea what issues matter most to my neighbors or how to talk about them"  
- "I get dozen newsletters a day from different activist groups and news sources — sometimes they say the exact same thing, sometimes slightly different. I don't know the source or what to prioritize"  
- "Precinct has 1,000 voters. Some live in houses, others in locked apartments I can’t easily enter. I need help knowing who to target, how to engage, and what to say"  
- "If I knew my precinct was 35% renters facing a housing crisis, I'd lead with that. But I have no way to know who is safe and who is in trouble"

**What we understand that others building in this space don't:** 

- Most campaign tech optimizes for operatives/campaigns. The real bottleneck is the precinct captain — the volunteer who has no time, no support, and no tools.  
- While no two Precinct Captains are alike, they are 100% volunteers and live in their own busy worlds. While they may be emotionally charged about the current political situation, it is difficult to stay motivated to do the hard ground work on a regular basis.  
- The diversity of precinct captains and their communication styles can be a thorn in the side of party leadership, so they are motivated to keep participation low to eliminate objection or competition. This tool can help facilitate how Precinct captains report real data back to party or candidate leadership.  
- Door-knock conversations work. Lit drops don't. But precinct captains default to lit drops because conversations are high-friction. The solution isn't to make them do more conversations; it's to make conversations low-friction.  
- Precinct captains aren't data scientists. They need one precinct brief per day, not a dashboard with 50 data sources. They need talking points, not raw voter data.  
- UNC voters are persuadable — but only if messaging is tailored to their cohort *and* their local context. Off-the-shelf messaging doesn't work.  
- Available GOTV tools often are expensive or use methods that are opaque, if they share anything at all. We use open source data that anyone can replicate and validate that what we’re doing is true.

## Appendix: Tech Stack \+ Execution

**Data Infrastructure:**

- Ohio Secretary of State statewide voter file (SWVF): 88 counties, \~7.9M voters, 135 columns (46 static \+ 89 election history)  
- Parquet (partitioned by county) for caching; DuckDB for queries  
- Polars for vectorized out-of-core processing (not pandas)  
- Cohort classifier: 26-year election history → Pure R/D, Crossover, UNC+lean prediction

**Outputs:**

- Web dashboard (Chart.js): county → city → precinct drill-down (live at precincts.info)  
- Excel workbooks: county-level stakeholder reports  
- JSON exports: precinct captain targeting CSVs (one file per cohort per precinct)  
- Narrative cards: templated briefings for different jurisdiction types

**Architecture Principles:**

- For github repositories, strict PII handling (names and addresses never committed; .gitignore enforces local-only)  
- For approved tool user access, customized walk-lists can be generated per precinct captain requirements given available processed criteria   
- Provenance preserved (parquet metadata tracks BoE timestamps, data source URLs)  
- Loud failures preferred (no silent degradation; errors logged, pipeline continues)  
- Threadpool-based parallelism (not multiprocessing) \+ orjson for performance  
- Prototypes will use commercial LLMaaS API-access before exploring self-hosted models

**Phases:**

- P1 ✓: Atomic ingestion (SWVF → Parquet cache)  
- P2 (in progress): Roll-up (precinct → city/ward/school district/legislative district)  
- P3 (in progress): Diffs \+ GIS (demographic/registration deltas; choropleth drill-down)  
- P4 (planned): Dark data (property \+ Census \+ USPS NCOA → unregistered residents for GOTV)  
- P5 (planned): Agentic workflow (autonomous content synthesis \+ targeting \+ conversation coaching \+ feedback loops)

**Timeline to revenue:**

- 2026 cycle: Proof-of-concept (Phase 1–3 live, Phase 4 beta, early customer cohort)  
- 2028 cycle: Production deployment (Phase 5 agentic layer, multi-state, SaaS model)
